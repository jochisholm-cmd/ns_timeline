The timeline for Nova Scotia's plan to come out of lockdown

The dates listed below are suggested timelines provided by the government, and are suspect to change depending on the available epidemiology at the time. To read more about the reopening plan, click here. This timeline was last updated June 1, 2021.

April 28

Nova Scotia goes into lockdown to beat back its third wave.

May 28

Premier Iain Rankin lays out reopening up plan; announces new restrictions being lifted across the province for Wednesday, June 2. Schools across the province, except for the HRM and Sydney area, will go back to in-person learning by June 2.

May 31

The following Monday, Rankin updates the reopening plan to include HRM and Sydney area schools. they will go back to in-person learning on Thursday, June 3.

June 2

Phase one of the province's reopening plan begins. Among the restrictions lifted are on patios for bars and restaurants, who will now be open to serve groups of 10 outside with masks being mandatory when not eating or drinking.
